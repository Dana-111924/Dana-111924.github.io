中国朝代 Chinese Dynasties

Historical Timeline

China's history spans over 5,000 years, with numerous dynasties that have shaped its culture, politics, and society. Below is a comprehensive overview of China's major dynasties and their significant contributions.

夏朝 Xia Dynasty (c. 2070 BCE – c. 1600 BCE)

Rulers (17)

Yu, Qi, Taikang, Zhongkang, Xiang, Shaokang, Zhu, Huai, Mang, Xie, Buxiang, Jiong, Jin, Kongjia, Gao, Fa, Jie

Major Events

  • Loss of state under Taikang (replaced by Hou Yi)
  • Revival under Shaokang

Notable Figures

大禹 Yu

Famous for controlling floods and uniting the tribes, founder of the first Chinese dynasty.

启 Qi

Son of Yu, established hereditary rule, marking the transition from tribal confederation to dynasty.

商朝 Shang Dynasty (c. 1600 BCE – 1046 BCE)

Rulers (30)

Tang, Waibing, Zhongren, Taijia, Woding, Taigeng, Xiaojia, Taiwu, Yongji, Zhongding, ... up to Emperor Zhou (Di Xin)

Major Events

  • Capital moved to Yin (Anyang)
  • Peak during Wu Ding's reign

Notable Figures

伊尹 Yi Yin

Founding prime minister, strategist who helped establish Shang.

妇好 Fu Hao

First recorded female military commander in China, wife of King Wu Ding.

周朝 Zhou Dynasty (1046 BCE – 256 BCE)

Rulers (12)

King Wu, King Cheng, King Kang, King Zhao, ... to King You

Major Events

  • Fall of Shang (Battle of Muye)
  • Feudal system implemented

Notable Figures

周武王 King Wu

Defeated Shang and established Zhou.

周公 Duke of Zhou

Formulated the rites and music systems, solidified Zhou governance.

东周 Eastern Zhou (770 BCE – 256 BCE)

春秋时期 Spring and Autumn Period (770 BCE – 476 BCE)

Features

Decline of Zhou central authority, emergence of regional hegemonies

Notable Figures

  • 孔子 Confucius: Founder of Confucianism, advocate of "Ren" and "Li"
  • 管仲 Guan Zhong: Prime minister of Qi, implemented economic and military reforms

战国时期 Warring States Period (475 BCE – 256 BCE)

Features

Seven powerful states competed, widespread reforms

Notable Figures

  • 商鞅 Shang Yang: Led Qin reforms; abolished feudalism, emphasized agriculture and military
  • 孟子 Mencius: Confucian scholar advocating benevolent governance

秦朝 Qin Dynasty (221 BCE – 207 BCE)

Rulers (3)

Qin Shi Huang, Huhai, Ziying

Major Events

  • Unified China
  • Standardized currency, script, and measurements

Notable Figures

秦始皇 Qin Shi Huang

First emperor; centralized power and began building the Great Wall

李斯 Li Si

Prime minister, key legalist advisor and architect of administrative reform

汉朝 Han Dynasty

西汉 Western Han (206 BCE – 8 CE)

Emperors (15)

Emperor Gaozu (Liu Bang), Emperor Hui (Liu Ying), Former Emperor Liu Gong, Later Emperor Liu Hong, Emperor Wen (Liu Heng), Emperor Jing (Liu Qi), Emperor Wu (Liu Che), Emperor Zhao (Liu Fuling), Prince of Changyi (Liu He), Emperor Xuan (Liu Xun), Emperor Yuan (Liu Shi), Emperor Cheng (Liu Ao), Emperor Ai (Liu Xin), Emperor Ping (Liu Kan), Infant Emperor (Liu Ying)

Major Historical Events

  • Chu–Han Contention (Liu Bang defeats Xiang Yu to establish Han)
  • Rule of Wen and Jing (peaceful recovery and economic prosperity)
  • Emperor Wu's northern expeditions against the Xiongnu
  • Zhang Qian's mission to the Western Regions (opened Silk Road)

Notable Figures

  • 刘邦 Liu Bang: Founder of the Han Dynasty, promoted peaceful recovery, and laid the foundation for 400 years of Han rule.
  • 汉武帝 Emperor Wu: Strengthened central authority, promoted Confucianism as state ideology, defeated Xiongnu, opened up Silk Road.
  • 司马迁 Sima Qian: Author of Records of the Grand Historian, the first comprehensive historical record in biographical form; both literary and historical in value.

东汉 Eastern Han (25 – 220 CE)

Emperors (14)

Emperor Guangwu (Liu Xiu), Emperor Ming (Liu Zhuang), Emperor Zhang (Liu Da), Emperor He (Liu Zhao), Emperor Shang (Liu Long), Emperor An (Liu Hu), Emperor Shun (Liu Bao), Emperor Chong (Liu Bing), Emperor Zhi (Liu Zuan), Emperor Huan (Liu Zhi), Emperor Ling (Liu Hong), Young Emperor (Liu Bian), Emperor Xian (Liu Xie)

Major Historical Events

  • Guangwu's restoration of the Han Dynasty
  • Partisan Prohibitions (struggles between officials and eunuchs)
  • Yellow Turban Rebellion (weakened imperial authority)

Notable Figures

  • 刘秀 Liu Xiu: Rebuilt the Han, reduced taxes, improved governance, ushered in the "Guangwu Restoration."
  • 班超 Ban Chao: Former scholar turned general, served in Western Regions for 31 years, reestablished Han influence and international exchange.

三国 Three Kingdoms (220 – 280 CE)

魏 Wei (220 – 265 CE, 5 Emperors)

Key Figures

曹操 Cao Cao

Founder of Wei, unified the north, introduced the Tuntian (military-agricultural) system, literary leader of the Jian'an period.

蜀 Shu (221 – 263 CE, 2 Emperors)

Key Figures

诸葛亮 Zhuge Liang

Prime Minister of Shu, implemented legal and governance reforms, led five northern expeditions, author of Memorial of the Expedition, symbol of loyalty.

吴 Wu (229 – 280 CE, 4 Emperors)

Key Figures

孙权 Sun Quan

Developed the South, dispatched Wei Wen to Taiwan, advanced economic and cultural growth in the southeast.

西晋 Western Jin (265 – 316 CE)

Emperors (4)

Sima Yan, Sima Zhong, Sima Chi, Sima Ye

Major Historical Events

  • Sima Yan's unification of China
  • War of the Eight Princes (internal conflict that led to downfall)

Notable Figures

司马炎 Sima Yan

Founded Western Jin, temporarily unified the country, implemented land reform (Equal-field system), but later neglected governance.

杜预 Du Yu

General who conquered Wu, scholar known for his annotations on Zuo's Commentary on the Spring and Autumn Annals.

东晋 Eastern Jin (317 – 420 CE)

Emperors (11)

Sima Rui, Sima Shao, Sima Yan, Sima Yue, Sima Dan, Sima Pi, Sima Yi, Sima Yu, Sima Yao, Sima Dezong, Sima Dewen

Major Historical Events

  • Southward migration of northern elites (cultural transfer and southern development)
  • Battle of Fei River (Eastern Jin's victory over Former Qin)

Notable Figures

王羲之 Wang Xizhi

Renowned calligrapher, creator of Preface to the Orchid Pavilion, redefined Chinese calligraphy.

谢安 Xie An

Eminent statesman, led defense at Fei River, secured Eastern Jin's survival.

南北朝 Northern and Southern Dynasties (420 – 589 CE)

Southern Dynasties

Song, Qi, Liang, Chen

刘裕 Liu Yu (Song)

Rose from humble origins, reformed politics.

萧统 Xiao Tong (Liang)

Compiled Wen Xuan, China's first major literary anthology.

Northern Dynasties

Northern Wei, Eastern Wei, Western Wei, Northern Qi, Northern Zhou

孝文帝 Emperor Xiaowen (Tuoba Hong)

Led major Sinicization reforms, moved capital to Luoyang.

隋朝 Sui Dynasty (581 – 618 CE)

Emperors (3)

Emperor Wen (Yang Jian), Emperor Yang (Yang Guang), Emperor Gong (Yang You)

Major Historical Events

  • Reunification of North and South (ended 300 years of division)
  • Initiated the imperial examination system
  • Dug the Grand Canal (enhanced north-south connectivity)

Notable Figures

隋文帝 Emperor Wen (Yang Jian)

Founded the Sui, implemented the Three Departments and Six Ministries system, reduced taxation, ushered in the "Kaihuang Golden Age."

李春 Li Chun

Designed the Zhaozhou Bridge, the oldest surviving stone arch bridge in the world—masterpiece of ancient engineering.

唐朝 Tang Dynasty (618 – 907 CE)

Emperors (21)

Li Yuan, Li Shimin, Li Zhi, Wu Zetian, Li Longji, etc.

Major Historical Events

  • Xuanwu Gate Incident (Li Shimin seized power)
  • Reign of Zhenguan (Golden Age of governance)
  • Kaiyuan Prosperity (Peak of Tang dynasty)
  • An Lushan Rebellion (start of decline)

Notable Figures

唐太宗 Emperor Taizong (Li Shimin)

Welcomed criticism, valued meritocracy, created the "Reign of Zhenguan," solidified Tang's international stature.

武则天 Empress Wu Zetian

Only female emperor in Chinese history, expanded the civil service exam, weakened aristocratic privilege, advanced social mobility.

李白 Li Bai & 杜甫 Du Fu

"Immortal of Poetry" (romanticist) and "Poet Sage" (realist), respectively, their works defined Tang poetry and shaped Chinese literature.

宋朝 Song Dynasty (960 – 1279 CE)

北宋 Northern Song (960 – 1127 CE, 9 Emperors)

Key Events

  • Chen Bridge Mutiny (Zhao Kuangyin founded Song)
  • Treaty of Chanyuan (peace with Liao)
  • Wang Anshi's Reforms (economic/military modernization attempt)

Key Figures

  • 赵匡胤 Zhao Kuangyin: Ended Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms, disbanded military threats, centralized power.
  • 包拯 Bao Zheng: Known for uprightness and fairness, a symbol of judicial justice—nicknamed "Justice Bao."
  • 沈括 Shen Kuo: Author of Dream Pool Essays, documented inventions like movable type and geomagnetism—milestone in Chinese science history.

南宋 Southern Song (1127 – 1279 CE, 9 Emperors)

Key Events

  • Yue Fei's resistance against the Jurchens
  • Battle of Yamen (marked the dynasty's fall)

Key Figures

  • 岳飞 Yue Fei: Famous general, fought to reclaim lost territory, revered for patriotism and loyalty.
  • 朱熹 Zhu Xi: Neo-Confucian philosopher, emphasized "investigation of things" and became the ideological backbone of Yuan, Ming, and Qing official thought.

元朝 Yuan Dynasty (1271 – 1368 CE)

Emperors (11)

Kublai Khan, Temür, Külüg, etc.

Major Historical Events

  • Kublai unified China
  • Introduced provincial administrative system
  • Marco Polo's travels to China

Notable Figures

忽必烈 Kublai Khan

Founded Yuan Dynasty, adopted Han governance, reopened the Grand Canal, fostered multiethnic unity.

郭守敬 Guo Shoujing

Astronomer, compiled the Shoushi Calendar, as accurate as the Gregorian calendar, developed astronomical instruments.

关汉卿 Guan Hanqing

Father of Yuan Drama, author of The Injustice to Dou E, exposed societal injustice and advanced dramatic literature.

明朝 Ming Dynasty (1368 – 1644 CE)

Emperors (16)

Zhu Yuanzhang, Zhu Di, Zhu Gaochi, Zhu Zhanji, etc.

Major Historical Events

  • Abolition of the Chancellor post
  • Zheng He's seven maritime expeditions
  • Zhang Juzheng's reforms (Single Whip Tax Law)
  • Uprising led by Li Zicheng

Notable Figures

朱元璋 Zhu Yuanzhang

Rose from poverty, founded Ming Dynasty, punished corruption, promoted peace and agriculture.

郑和 Zheng He

Led seven voyages across Asia and Africa, boosted maritime trade and cross-cultural exchange, symbol of naval power.

李时珍 Li Shizhen

Wrote Compendium of Materia Medica, recorded 1,892 medicinal substances, translated worldwide, influential in global medicine.

清朝 Qing Dynasty (1636 – 1912 CE)

Emperors (10)

Hong Taiji, Fulin, Xuanye, Yinzhen, Hongli, etc.

Major Historical Events

  • Reign of Kangxi and Qianlong (Golden Age)
  • First Opium War (start of modern Chinese history)
  • Hundred Days' Reform (failed modernization attempt)

Notable Figures

康熙 Emperor Kangxi

Suppressed rebellions, reclaimed Taiwan, led campaigns in Mongolia, secured unity of the empire.

曹雪芹 Cao Xueqin

Author of Dream of the Red Chamber, masterpiece of Chinese literature reflecting society through family decline.

林则徐 Lin Zexu

Ordered the destruction of opium in 1839, symbol of resistance to British opium trade, pivotal in awakening modern Chinese consciousness.

中华民国 Republic of China (1912 – 1949)

National Leaders

Over this 38-year period, there were 22 successive heads of state. Among the most well-known were Sun Yat-sen and Yuan Shikai.

Major Historical Events

  • Xinhai Revolution (1911): Overthrew the monarchy and established Asia's first democratic republic.
  • May Fourth Movement (1919): A patriotic campaign against imperialism and feudalism; catalyzed the New Culture Movement.
  • War of Resistance Against Japan (1937–1945): Nationwide resistance against Japanese invasion, safeguarding national sovereignty.
  • Chinese Civil War (1945–1949): Culminated in the fall of the Nationalist government and the establishment of a new regime.

Notable Figures

孙中山 Sun Yat-sen

Forefather of modern China, proposed the Three Principles of the People, led the 1911 revolution, and ended the imperial system.

鲁迅 Lu Xun

Iconic writer and thinker; used powerful literature to critique the old system, awaken national consciousness, and lead the New Culture Movement.

毛泽东 Mao Zedong

Led the Communist Party to victory in the civil war, pivotal in founding the new China and reshaping its trajectory.

中华人民共和国 People's Republic of China (1949 – Present)

National Leaders

Presidents of the PRC from 1949 to today include Mao Zedong, Liu Shaoqi, Soong Ching-ling (Honorary President), Li Xiannian, Yang Shangkun, Jiang Zemin, Hu Jintao, and Xi Jinping.

Major Historical Events

  • Founding of the PRC (1949): Marked the beginning of a new era where the people held political power.
  • Reform and Opening Up (starting 1978): Unleashed rapid economic growth and opened China to the world.
  • Return of Hong Kong and Macao (1997 & 1999): Key milestones in achieving national reunification.
  • Poverty Alleviation Campaign: Historically resolved absolute poverty across the country.
  • Completion of a Moderately Prosperous Society: Achieved the first centennial goal of national development.

Notable Figures

邓小平 Deng Xiaoping

Architect of reform and opening up; proposed the concept of One Country, Two Systems; charted China's path toward socialism with Chinese characteristics.

袁隆平 Yuan Longping

"Father of Hybrid Rice," developed high-yield rice strains, significantly contributing to global food security.

屠呦呦 Tu Youyou

Pharmacologist who discovered artemisinin, reducing global malaria deaths; awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine, elevating traditional Chinese medicine on the global stage.
